Buddhist Cosmology and Ecology Research

Gornall, Alastair. “Mapping the Moral Cosmos: Early Modern Buddhist Cosmographies in Southeast Asia.” History of Religions 63, no. 3 (2024): 289–321.
View online
This article examines early modern Pali cosmographical texts from Myanmar and Thailand, exploring how they re-imagined the moral structure of the world through Buddhist ethical cosmology and local geographical imagination.
Gornall, Alastair. “The Light on the Six Realms: Ethics and Rebirth in the Chagatidīpanīṭīkā.” Journal of the Pali Text Society 44 (2025): 57–94.
Publisher link
A critical edition and translation of the Chagatidīpanīṭīkā, one of the earliest Southeast Asian works on Buddhist ethics, situating it within broader intellectual developments in Theravāda scholasticism.
Gornall, Alastair. “Ecological Thought in Pre-colonial Buddhist Southeast Asia.” Religions 16, no. 4 (2025).
This paper reconstructs early Southeast Asian Buddhist conceptions of the natural environment, showing how cosmological and moral ideas shaped pre-modern ecological reasoning and human–environment relations.
